Give your AI assistant
a sense of Scripture.

Resonate runs as an MCP server — a small tool your assistant can call. Add it once, and Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Cursor or any MCP-speaking app can reach for a verified verse, weave a story, or pull reels — mid-conversation, on its own. You don't change your chat app; you hand it a new sense.

You type a real moment → Your assistant calls Resonate → A verified verse comes back (never invented)

Add it to your assistant

Pick your app. The engine runs on your machine, keeps no transcript, and stores only theme patterns — never what you wrote.

1

Open the config

Claude Desktop → Settings → Developer → Edit Config. It opens claude_desktop_config.json.

2

Add the Resonate server

Paste this inside mcpServers (use the full path to the file on your machine):

"mcpServers": {
  "resonate": {
    "command": "python",
    "args": ["C:/path/to/resonate/integrations/mcp/resonate_mcp.py"]
  }
}
3

Restart Claude

You'll see a 🔨 tool icon. Now say "I'm exhausted and losing hope" — Claude will call resonate_verse and answer with a real, cited verse.

What your assistant can now do

Four tools, the same guarantees as everywhere else — safety first, verses verbatim, nothing stored.
resonate_verse

A moment → one verified verse, or an honest silence. Crisis → help, never a verse.

generate_story

Weaves the moment into a vetted biblical narrative, labeled — ending on a verbatim verse.

reel_groups

Prioritized sets of story reels for the moment and the threads you return to.

fetch_passage

Verbatim licensed passage text by reference, straight from YouVersion.

Why a tool, not a chatbot? Because a billion people already pour their real lives into an AI — not a Bible app. Resonate meets them there: it teaches the assistant they already trust to reach for Scripture at the right moment, with the words verified and a human-help card when it hears darkness. That's the whole idea — Scripture, where you already are.
