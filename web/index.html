The problem

The most honest words of our time are typed to an AI.

Grief at 2 a.m. Exam panic. "I feel like I'm failing everyone." Billions of these sentences are poured into chatbots every day — not into a Bible app, not to a pastor. Scripture has never been present in that room. Not because it doesn't belong there — because no one built the bridge.

What it is

Three quiet answers, one engine.

I

A quiet verse

A parchment card slips in beside your chat when your words echo a verse — verified text, never a model's memory. Ask something ordinary and it stays silent. Ignore it, and it folds itself into a small wax seal. It waits better than we do.

R
II

Your story

One tap weaves your moment into a real biblical narrative — Elijah under the broom tree for the burned-out, Peter on the water for the afraid — clearly labeled a reflection, never invented Scripture, always ending on the verse, verbatim.

III

Story reels

Every verse carries a doorway — a short vertical story film of that passage, the format we pitch for YouVersion and partners to host. The moment moves from a popup… to something you carry.

How it hears

A context engine between two APIs.

Gloo AI reads the emotional beats. Our engine matches them against a curated, theme-tagged shortlist — hybrid retrieval fused by reciprocal rank, re-ranked by tone and by a memory of the themes you keep returning to. YouVersion supplies the words. Nothing is ever hallucinated.

Gloo · emotional beats→ engine · hybrid retrieval + RRF→ memory · your recurring themes→ policy · speak or stay silent→ YouVersion · the verbatim verse
And one rule above all of it: when someone is in real darkness, Resonate never answers with a verse. It goes quiet, and points to people who can help.

Beyond the extension

Scripture as a capability — for any AI assistant.

Resonate ships as an MCP server. Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — any assistant that speaks the Model Context Protocol can call the same engine natively, with every guarantee enforced on the server: safety first, verbatim verses only, stories labeled, memory that spans surfaces.

// Claude Desktop — claude_desktop_config.json 
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "resonate": {
      "command": "python",
      "args": ["path/to/resonate/integrations/mcp/resonate_mcp.py"]
    }
  }
}
# Claude Code 
claude mcp add resonate -- python integrations/mcp/resonate_mcp.py
resonate_versea verified verse echoing the moment — or an honest "stay silent"; crisis input returns help, never a verse
generate_storythe person's moment woven into one vetted biblical narrative, labeled, ending on the verbatim verse
fetch_passagelicensed passage text by reference — quoted exactly, never paraphrased
